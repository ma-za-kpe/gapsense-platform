## Ghana-Specific Context

### Classroom Reality
- **Class size:** 40-60 students (limited individual attention)
- **Resources:** Minimal (chalkboard, limited lab equipment, shared textbooks)
- **Lab access:** Limited (many schools lack functional science labs)
- **Assessment stakes:** WASSCE required for university (high-stakes)
- **Language:** English is L2 for 90%+ of students (affects comprehension)
- **Practical work:** Often demonstrated by teacher, not hands-on for students

### Common Challenges in Ghana Science Education
1. **Conceptual Understanding** - Rote memorization without understanding mechanisms
2. **Practical Skills** - Limited hands-on experience due to large classes and resource constraints
3. **Abstract Concepts** - Difficulty visualizing atoms, molecules, cells, body systems
4. **Calculations** - Weak math foundations affect stoichiometry, circuits, motion problems
5. **Graph Interpretation** - Can plot but struggle with interpretation and analysis
6. **Equipment Use** - Lack of experience with lab equipment (microscopes, meters, etc.)

### Interventions Must Be:
- Demonstration-based (1 teacher shows, 40-60 students observe)
- Resource-efficient (minimal equipment, improvised apparatus)
- Safety-conscious (limited supervision requires emphasis on safety)
- Culturally relevant (Ghana contexts, local examples)
- WASSCE-aligned (exam preparation focus)

## Content Standards Coding Format

**Format: X.Y.Z.CS.N**

Where:
- **X** = Year (1, 2, or 3)
- **Y** = Strand (1-4)
- **Z** = Sub-strand (1-3)
- **CS** = Content Standard (literal)
- **N** = Sequential number (1, 2, 3...)

**Examples:**
- `1.1.1.CS.1` = Year 1, Strand 1 (Exploring Materials), Sub-strand 1 (Science and Materials), Content Standard 1
- `2.3.2.CS.1` = Year 2, Strand 3 (Vigour Behind Life), Sub-strand 2 (Forces), Content Standard 1
- `3.4.1.CS.2` = Year 3, Strand 4 (Relationships), Sub-strand 1 (Human Body & Health), Content Standard 2
